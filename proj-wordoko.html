Wordoko UI

A Mobile Platform for Learning Japanese

Introduction

Wordoko is a word puzzle app designed to practice the three Japanese writing systems, Kanji, Katakana, and Hiragana. The idea was inspired by the present decline of Kanji used amongst the younger generations in Japan. Wordoko is a fun and entertaining way for Japanese language learners to practice their comprehension of the three writing systems.

My Role

Working with the design studio, Great Job, I was responsible for the visual look of the game, as well as the branding and website.

The Constraints

  • UI design must comply to iOS design guidelines
  • Designs must fit screen dimensions for Iphone 4 & 5
  • All visual elements should follow Great Job's aesthetic and design philosophies
  • Each puzzle is in either Kanji, Hiragana, or Katakana. The words in the word bank are then in English and the other two writing systems

Keep It Simple

My approach focused on clear communication to the player on the different functions and navigation of the app. The design was minimal, using color to denote the different categories, and the icon elements to indicate the screen options. The main typeface for the app was Avenir Next, a screen-friendly version of Avenir.

Gesture Based Design

To select a word on the puzzle, the player needs to touch and hold to highlight letters. The element at the bottom of the interface is the word bank. The arrows indicate to the player that they can swipe left or right to view the various words they are searching for in the puzzle. The line above the word signals to the player that they can swipe up revealing the word in English and other two Japanese writing systems(depending on the system chosen as the puzzle).

Give Hints, Not Answers

The key icon on the top right was created as a hint section. The player if stumped can press and hold the key icon to reveal answers. It was designed to only be viewed briefly instead of being just an answer key, so as to not make the puzzle too easy for the player.
